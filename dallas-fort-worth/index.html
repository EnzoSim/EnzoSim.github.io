Where to stay

For this trip, the best neighborhood is the one that removes friction after midnight: close to Katy Trail, Klyde Warren Park, the Arts District, rooftops, cocktail bars, and short rides to Deep Ellum or Bishop Arts.

Best base

Uptown / Harwood

Polished, walkable, bars everywhere.

Stay here if Friday and Saturday nights matter. You can start with Happiest Hour, walk Katy Trail, use the M-Line trolley, and keep most rides short.

Design District

Better hotel scene, less natural walking.

Book it if the pool/design hotel matters more than stepping straight into Uptown. It works, but assume rideshares for most nights.

Tickets & timing

Checked online on June 7, 2026. This is the compact booking desk: what to buy, what is free, and what can wreck the plan if you forget the hours.

Book before the trip

  • Sixth Floor Museum Wed-Sun 10am-5pm, last entry 4:15pm, adult $27. Timed tickets
  • Nasher Sculpture Center Wed-Sun 11am-5pm, adult $10. Plan visit
  • Dallas Arboretum daily 9am-5pm, adults $21.95 weekday / $25.95 weekend, parking $15. Tickets
  • Stockyards Championship Rodeo Friday/Saturday 7:30pm, doors 6pm. AXS tickets
  • Billy Bob's events vary; Friday/Saturday open until 2am. Events
  • Las Colinas gondola classic cruise from $155 for 2, daily by reservation. Reserve

Free or low-friction

  • Dallas Museum of Art Wed-Sun 11am-5pm, free general admission but still reserve a GA ticket. Reserve
  • Klyde Warren Park daily 6am-11pm, free.
  • M-Line Trolley free/name-your-fare, Friday/Saturday until midnight, Sunday until 10pm.
  • Katy Trail 5am-11pm, best at golden hour.
  • Fort Worth Herd daily 11:30am and 4pm, free, weather permitting.
  • Amon Carter free; Kimbell permanent collection free.

Do not miss

  • Monday trap: Sixth Floor, DMA, Nasher, Kimbell, Modern, and Amon Carter are closed Monday.
  • Fort Worth night: rodeo starts 7:30pm; Billy Bob's bull riding is separate and often cash add-on.
  • TRE: good for daytime, risky late. Check the last eastbound train before committing.
  • Clubs: verify event tickets and 21+ ID rules for It'll Do, The Nines, Round-Up, and Waterproof.
  • Irving: Ruth Paine House requires guided tour reservations at least 24 hours ahead.

The 3-day plan

Built for a 10pm Friday arrival, two clubbing nights, one Fort Worth day trip, and no dead-weight hiking detours.

Frinight

Arrive, check in, go straight out

  1. 10:00pm Land, rideshare to Uptown. If the final hotel is Canopy, you are in the Cityplace / West Village pocket; if Marriott, you are closer to Harwood and the Arts District.
  2. 11:15pm First drink: Happiest Hour if you want big rooftop energy, Waterproof if you want the dressed-up Statler roof, Midnight Rambler if you want cocktails first.
  3. 12:15am Club route: It'll Do if the DJ is right, The Nines for Deep Ellum, or Round-Up if you want country dancing without waiting for Fort Worth.

Do not book a serious dinner Friday. Check the club calendar before landing, then commit to one late-night destination instead of bouncing across town.

Satday + late

Fort Worth, then choose your night

  1. 10:30am Leave Dallas by car or TRE. Driving is cleaner if you plan to stay late after the rodeo or Billy Bob's.
  2. 12:00pm Cultural District: Kimbell permanent collection is free; Modern is $16 except free Fridays; Amon Carter is free.
  3. 4:00pm Fort Worth Herd cattle drive on East Exchange Avenue. It is free and weather permitting.
  4. 6:00pm Doors for the Stockyards Championship Rodeo. Rodeo starts Friday/Saturday at 7:30pm.
  5. 10:00pm Billy Bob's if you want the honky-tonk late night; otherwise rideshare back and pick one Dallas club.

Stay for Billy Bob's Texas if you want the honky-tonk version. If you miss the last TRE, budget for a late rideshare back to Dallas.

SunDallas

Dallas core, with an Irving/Las Colinas option

  1. 10:30am Bishop Arts brunch: Written By The Seasons is the Michelin-listed fit.
  2. 12:30pm Arts District: DMA free GA, Nasher ticketed, Klyde Warren as the connector, M-Line trolley back toward Uptown.
  3. 4:30pm Option A: stay Dallas for Katy Trail / Lower Greenville. Option B: go to Irving/Las Colinas for Mustangs, Mandalay Canal, and a reserved gondola.
  4. 7:30pm Dinner: Tei-An, Gemma, Mamani, or Written By The Seasons if you stayed in Bishop Arts. If Irving, watch Toyota Music Factory's event calendar.

Irving is not the main trip, but it is a good golden-hour add-on if the canals/gondola or a Toyota Music Factory show line up.

Monexit

One clean final morning

  1. 9:30am Pretty version: Dallas Arboretum, open daily 9am-5pm. Easy version: Katy Trail coffee walk from Uptown.
  2. 11:30am Lunch near the hotel, pack, airport.

Monday is a museum-closure minefield: do not plan Sixth Floor, DMA, Nasher, Kimbell, Modern, or Amon Carter for the final morning.

05

Dallas, worth doing

Keep the weekend urban: walks, rooftops, galleries, restaurants, and one big Fort Worth detour. Skip the regional hiking/VTT layer this time.

01

The Arts District must

Dallas Museum of Art, Nasher Sculpture Center, Meyerson, Winspear, and Klyde Warren next door. This is the cleanest culture-and-walk block.

02

Klyde Warren Park free

A deck park over the freeway, stitching Uptown to downtown. Best as a transition rather than a destination.

03

Katy Trail + M-Line easy

Golden-hour walk or bike on Katy Trail, then use the free McKinney Avenue trolley as the original Dallas move.

04

Bishop Arts half-day

Independent shops, murals, coffee, and a softer Sunday pace. Pair it with Written By The Seasons.

05

Deep Ellum evening

Murals by day, clubs and live music after dark. Go with intention, then rideshare back.

06

Skip the hikes

Cedar Hill State Park, Cedar Ridge Preserve, and Boulder Park are good regional options, but wrong for a late-arrival, nightlife-heavy first Dallas weekend.

06

Irving, if you mean Irvine

In DFW, the useful version is Irving / Las Colinas. It is not a must-see over Fort Worth, but it can be a polished 2-4 hour add-on from Uptown if you want canals, a gondola, or a concert.

Best use

Golden-hour canal plan

Go late Sunday: Mustangs of Las Colinas, Mandalay Canal walk, gondola if reserved, then Toyota Music Factory only if an event is worth it.

Skip if

You only have one culture slot

Dallas Arts District and Fort Worth Cultural District both outrank Irving if the weekend starts late and you are clubbing twice.

Micro-history

Ruth Paine House

Worth it only if JFK history genuinely matters to you. Tours are Tue-Fri at 10am and 2pm, reservation required 24 hours ahead.

08

Fort Worth, the day west

Guide du Routard is right to separate Fort Worth from Dallas. The Stockyards, Cultural District, Sundance Square, and rodeo/honky-tonk culture make it feel like a real second city, not a suburb.

10:30Leave Dallas from Canopy or Marriott
12:00Kimbell / Modern / Amon Carter
16:00Free Herd cattle drive
19:30Rodeo at Cowtown Coliseum
LateBilly Bob's or return to Dallas
Recommended Saturday

Culture-first route

Leave after a slow morning, do one museum properly, hit the 4pm cattle drive, dinner, then rodeo. This is the most balanced version.

If you wake early

Stockyards-first route

Make the 11:30am cattle drive, lunch, guided Stockyards walk, then Cultural District. Better if the rodeo is not the night priority.

If you stay late

Honky-tonk route

Rodeo 7:30pm, Billy Bob's after. Treat the return as a rideshare unless the TRE schedule clearly works for your exact night.

Stockyards + cattle drive

The headline western move: longhorns on East Exchange Avenue at 11:30am and 4pm, free and weather permitting, then saloons, bootmakers, and the old Livestock Exchange.

Kimbell or The Modern

Kimbell is free for the permanent collection; The Modern is $16 but free Fridays; Amon Carter is free. Pick one, not all three, if you slept late.

Billy Bob's option

Stay late if you want the honky-tonk version. Billy Bob's is open until 2am Friday/Saturday; concerts and bull riding are separate event decisions.
