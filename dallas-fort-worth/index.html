Where to stay

For this trip, the best neighborhood is the one that removes friction after midnight: close to Katy Trail, Klyde Warren Park, the Arts District, rooftops, cocktail bars, and short rides to Deep Ellum or Bishop Arts.

Best base

Uptown / Harwood

Polished, walkable, bars everywhere.

Stay here if Friday and Saturday nights matter. You can start with Happiest Hour, walk Katy Trail, use the M-Line trolley, and keep most rides short.

Tickets & timing

Checked online on June 7, 2026. This is the compact booking desk: what to buy, what is free, and what can wreck the plan if you forget the hours. If you want Irving and Fort Worth by train on the same day, make it Saturday.

Book before the trip

  • Sixth Floor Museum Wed-Sun 10am-5pm, last entry 4:15pm, adult $27. Timed tickets
  • Nasher Sculpture Center Wed-Sun 11am-5pm, adult $10. Plan visit
  • Dallas Arboretum daily 9am-5pm, adults $21.95 weekday / $25.95 weekend, parking $15. Tickets
  • Stockyards Championship Rodeo Friday/Saturday 7:30pm, doors 6pm. AXS tickets
  • Billy Bob's events vary; Friday/Saturday open until 2am. Events
  • Las Colinas gondola classic cruise from $155 for 2, daily by reservation. Reserve

Free or low-friction

  • Dallas Museum of Art Wed-Sun 11am-5pm, free general admission but still reserve a GA ticket. Reserve
  • Klyde Warren Park daily 6am-11pm, free.
  • M-Line Trolley free/name-your-fare, Friday/Saturday until midnight, Sunday until 10pm.
  • Katy Trail 5am-11pm, best at golden hour.
  • Fort Worth Herd daily 11:30am and 4pm, free, weather permitting.
  • Amon Carter free; Kimbell permanent collection free.

Do not miss

  • Monday trap: Sixth Floor, DMA, Nasher, Kimbell, Modern, and Amon Carter are closed Monday.
  • Fort Worth night: rodeo starts 7:30pm; Billy Bob's bull riding is separate and often cash add-on.
  • TRE: regular service runs Monday-Saturday, not Sunday. Saturday is the train day for Irving + Fort Worth.
  • Clubs: verify event tickets and 21+ ID rules for It'll Do, The Nines, Round-Up, and Waterproof.
  • Irving: keep it tight before Fort Worth: Mustangs + canal, gondola only if timing is protected.
03

The 3-day plan

Built for a 10pm Friday arrival, two clubbing nights, one Saturday train day combining Irving/Las Colinas and Fort Worth, then a lighter Dallas Sunday.

Frinight

Arrive, check in, go straight out

  1. 10:00pm Land, rideshare to Uptown. If the final hotel is Canopy, you are in the Cityplace / West Village pocket; if Marriott, you are closer to Harwood and the Arts District.
  2. 11:15pm First drink: Happiest Hour if you want big rooftop energy, Waterproof if you want the dressed-up Statler roof, Midnight Rambler if you want cocktails first.
  3. 12:15am Club route: It'll Do if the DJ is right, The Nines for Deep Ellum, or Round-Up if you want country dancing without waiting for Fort Worth.

Do not book a serious dinner Friday. Check the club calendar before landing, then commit to one late-night destination instead of bouncing across town.

Satday + late

Irving + Fort Worth by train

  1. 9:30am DART Orange Line toward Las Colinas Urban Center. From Canopy, Cityplace/Uptown is the cleanest rail start; from Marriott, rideshare or trolley to a rail stop.
  2. 10:15am Irving/Las Colinas: Mustangs, Mandalay Canal walk, quick coffee. Only do a gondola if it is booked early and does not threaten the Fort Worth transfer.
  3. 12:15pm DART back toward Dallas, transfer to TRE westbound at Victory/Union depending on the exact schedule.
  4. 2:00pm Fort Worth: choose one cultural stop or go straight to the Stockyards. Do not try to do every museum on this combined day.
  5. 4:00pm Fort Worth Herd cattle drive on East Exchange Avenue. Free, weather permitting.
  6. 7:30pm Rodeo at Cowtown Coliseum, then Billy Bob's if you want the late honky-tonk version. Check the last TRE; rideshare back may be the real plan.

Stay for Billy Bob's Texas if you want the honky-tonk version. If you miss the last TRE, budget for a late rideshare back to Dallas.

SunDallas

Dallas recovery day

  1. 10:30am Bishop Arts brunch: Written By The Seasons is the Michelin-listed fit.
  2. 12:30pm Arts District: DMA free GA, Nasher ticketed, Klyde Warren as the connector, M-Line trolley back toward Uptown.
  3. 4:30pm Katy Trail, Lower Greenville, or Dallas Arboretum if you want a softer outdoor block.
  4. 7:30pm Dinner: Tei-An, Gemma, Mamani, or Written By The Seasons if you stayed in Bishop Arts.

Sunday should recover from the Saturday rail day. Keep it Dallas-only unless a specific Toyota Music Factory event is more important than rest.

Monexit

One clean final morning

  1. 9:30am Pretty version: Dallas Arboretum, open daily 9am-5pm. Easy version: Katy Trail coffee walk from Uptown.
  2. 11:30am Lunch near the hotel, pack, airport.

Monday is a museum-closure minefield: do not plan Sixth Floor, DMA, Nasher, Kimbell, Modern, or Amon Carter for the final morning.

Dallas, worth doing

Keep the weekend urban: walks, rooftops, galleries, restaurants, and one big Fort Worth detour. Skip the regional hiking/VTT layer this time.

Visit Dallas check Arts District, Klyde, Bishop Arts, Deep Ellum stay.

The official Dallas tourism framing backs a city weekend built around culture, green-space transitions, neighborhoods, food, and nightlife rather than regional hiking.

Visit Dallas
Visit Irving check Las Colinas is worth a tight morning, not a full day.

Mandalay Canal, Lake Carolyn, Mustangs, and Toyota Music Factory are the useful hits. The plan keeps them compact before the TRE transfer.

Visit Irving
Visit Fort Worth check Stockyards + culture district is the right westbound move.

The official Fort Worth angle points to Stockyards, live music/rodeo, and major museums, which matches the Saturday rail day.

Visit Fort Worth
01

The Arts District must

Dallas Museum of Art, Nasher Sculpture Center, Meyerson, Winspear, and Klyde Warren next door. This is the cleanest culture-and-walk block.

02

Klyde Warren Park free

A deck park over the freeway, stitching Uptown to downtown. Best as a transition rather than a destination.

03

Katy Trail + M-Line easy

Golden-hour walk or bike on Katy Trail, then use the free McKinney Avenue trolley as the original Dallas move.

04

Bishop Arts half-day

Independent shops, murals, coffee, and a softer Sunday pace. Pair it with Written By The Seasons.

05

Deep Ellum evening

Murals by day, clubs and live music after dark. Go with intention, then rideshare back.

06

Skip the hikes

Cedar Hill State Park, Cedar Ridge Preserve, and Boulder Park are good regional options, but wrong for a late-arrival, nightlife-heavy first Dallas weekend.

06

Irving / Las Colinas

This is the first half of Saturday, not a separate day. Keep Irving tight: a rail stop, a few good photos, maybe a short canal moment, then move toward TRE for Fort Worth.

Best use

Saturday morning opener

Do Mustangs of Las Colinas and Mandalay Canal as a compact 75-100 minute stop before heading back toward the TRE transfer.

Skip if

The gondola runs long

A gondola is only worth it if booked early enough to protect the Fort Worth cattle drive and rodeo timing.

Do not add

Ruth Paine House

Interesting, but wrong for this weekend plan: guided tours are Tue-Fri, and it would break the Saturday Irving + Fort Worth rail rhythm.

Photo stop Mustangs of Las Colinas

Do this. It is distinctive, free, right by the rail/canal area, and gives Irving a real point instead of feeling like a random suburb stop.

Walk Mandalay Canal + Lake Carolyn

Short waterfront loop, good for a coffee walk. Keep it to 25-35 minutes if Fort Worth is still the main event.

Only if booked early Gondola Adventures

Worth it romantically, but it consumes the schedule. Book the earliest practical slot or skip.

Backup culture Irving Arts Center

Legit gallery backup if the weather is bad. Good, but less iconic than Mustangs + canal for this specific trip.

Fort Worth, the day west

Guide du Routard is right to separate Fort Worth from Dallas. Since the plan also includes Irving, keep Fort Worth focused: Stockyards, 4pm cattle drive, rodeo, and Billy Bob's if the return works.

09:30DART toward Las Colinas
12:15Transfer back to TRE west
16:00Free Herd cattle drive
19:30Rodeo at Cowtown Coliseum
LateBilly Bob's or return to Dallas
Recommended Saturday

Irving-first route

Las Colinas in the morning, TRE west after lunch, Stockyards by mid-afternoon, cattle drive at 4pm, rodeo at 7:30pm.

If timing slips

Cut the museum

On a combined Irving + Fort Worth day, museums are optional. The non-negotiable Fort Worth anchor is the Stockyards / cattle drive / rodeo block.

If you stay late

Honky-tonk route

Rodeo 7:30pm, Billy Bob's after. Treat the return as a rideshare unless the Saturday TRE schedule clearly works for your exact night.

Stockyards + cattle drive

The headline western move: longhorns on East Exchange Avenue at 11:30am and 4pm, free and weather permitting, then saloons, bootmakers, and the old Livestock Exchange.

Kimbell or The Modern

Kimbell is free for the permanent collection; The Modern is $16 but free Fridays; Amon Carter is free. Pick one, not all three, if you slept late.

Billy Bob's option

Stay late if you want the honky-tonk version. Billy Bob's is open until 2am Friday/Saturday; concerts and bull riding are separate event decisions.

Eat, drink & coffee

For the Saturday train day, choose places that sit near Las Colinas or the Stockyards. Dallas Michelin anchors stay below for Sunday or for a cleaner dinner night.

Irving / Las Colinas

Use before the TRE transfer

  • The Ranch at Las ColinasTexas ranch-to-table anchor; best if you want a real meal before moving west.
  • Via RealLas Colinas classic for Southwestern/Mexican, more polished than casual.
  • Mr MaxTiny Japanese izakaya energy, the niche pick if you want something less obvious.
  • Empa MundoCasual local empanadas; good if you want fast, cheap, and not generic.
