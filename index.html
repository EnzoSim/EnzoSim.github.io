Research

Do water meters change household behavior?

Thesis in progress Behavioral Economics Policy Design Public Economics

My thesis asks whether residential water metering changes consumption and whether pricing or informational cues meaningfully alter household response.

Question

When metering makes marginal cost more visible, do households update behavior materially, or do frictions blunt the signal?

Why it matters

Water pricing is a useful case for understanding how infrastructure, information, and incentives interact in public services.

Supervisors: Justin Leroux and Jean-Luc Martel.
