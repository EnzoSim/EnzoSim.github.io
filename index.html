About / Current focus

Microeconomic theory matters most when it changes what institutions actually do.

I am drawn to questions where incentives, market structure, and behavioral frictions shape real outcomes: why households miss price signals, how merger analysis holds up under uncertainty, and what makes a policy intervention actually stick.

That interest runs through my thesis on residential water metering in Quebec, my merger work at Canada’s Competition Bureau, and my consulting projects at KPMG, where analysis had to become something decision-makers could use.
