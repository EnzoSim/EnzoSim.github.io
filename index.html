Research

Volumetric Water Pricing in Québec — Benefit Assessment

Master's Thesis · HEC Montréal · 2025-2026

Analyzing the economic benefits of volumetric water pricing systems using panel data from Québec municipalities and households. The research employs difference-in-differences methodology with staggered adoption to measure consumption effects, combined with heterogeneity analysis to identify differential impacts across demographic groups. Consumer surplus estimation quantifies welfare effects to inform tariff design recommendations that balance efficiency and equity objectives in public utility pricing.
