About

I'm drawn to questions where microeconomic theory meets real-world friction — why households ignore price signals, how market definition shapes merger outcomes, and what makes a nudge actually work. My thesis explores these ideas through residential water metering in Québec.

Outside the classroom I've worked on merger reviews at Canada's Competition Bureau and built economic-impact models at KPMG. I'm looking for roles where applied economics drives decisions — in consulting, policy, or tech.
